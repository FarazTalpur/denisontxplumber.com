Frequently Asked Questions

Signs of a burst pipe include unexplained water puddles, unusually high water bills, decreased water pressure, and water stains on walls or ceilings. You may also hear running water when all faucets are off.

First, turn off the main water supply to prevent further flooding. Then, turn off the electricity if water is near electrical outlets. Finally, contact a professional plumber to assess and repair the damage.

Prevent burst pipes by insulating them, keeping your home warm, letting faucets drip during freezing weather, sealing leaks, and disconnecting outdoor hoses. Itâ€™s also important to maintain a consistent thermostat setting.
