Frequently Asked Questions

Before installing an irrigation system, consider factors such as the type of plants in your garden, the size and shape of your landscape, local climate, water source availability, soil type, and any local regulations or restrictions on water usage.

It is recommended to have your irrigation system inspected and maintained at least once a year, preferably at the start of the growing season, to ensure it operates efficiently and to extend its lifespan.

Common signs include uneven watering, low water pressure, unusually high water bills, visible leaks, clogged sprinklers, and dry spots in the landscape. If you notice these, it might be time to call a professional for repairs.

While DIY installation is possible for simpler systems, larger or more complex systems often require professional installation to ensure proper design, coverage, and efficiency. Professionals can also navigate municipal codes and permits.

Smart irrigation systems can save water by adjusting watering schedules based on local weather conditions, soil moisture levels, and plant needs. This leads to more efficient water use, healthier plants, and potential cost savings on water bills.
