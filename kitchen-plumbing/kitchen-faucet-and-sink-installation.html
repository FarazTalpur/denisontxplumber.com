Frequently Asked Questions

To install a kitchen faucet and sink, you will typically need a basin wrench, adjustable wrench, plumber's putty, Teflon tape, bucket, and a sponge. You may need additional materials depending on the plumbing configuration, such as supply lines and a drain assembly kit.

To prepare for installation, first turn off the water supply and disconnect the water lines. Remove the old sink and faucet if present. Make sure to clean the area where the new sink will be placed. Check that your new sink and faucet fit the existing cutout or make necessary adjustments.

While many homeowners choose to install kitchen sinks and faucets themselves, hiring a plumber can ensure the job is done correctly, especially if you're dealing with complicated plumbing conditions or upgrades to water lines and drainage systems.

The general steps for installing a kitchen faucet include preparing the faucet components, inserting the faucet into the corresponding sink holes, securing it with mounting nuts, connecting the water supply lines, and tightening all connections. Finally, check for leaks and make adjustments if necessary.

To prevent leaks, ensure all connections are securely tightened and use plumber's putty or Teflon tape where appropriate, particularly on threaded connections. After installation, test the setup by running water and checking under the sink for any signs of leakage.
