Understanding Water Heater Maintenance

Proper water heater maintenance extends beyond simple checks. Key services include:

  • Anode Rod Inspection : Essential for preventing corrosion and prolonging heater lifespan.
  • Sediment Flushing : Vital for efficiency, this process involves removing built-up sediment that impedes heating.

Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to flush your water heater at least once a year to remove sediment buildup and maintain efficiency.

Noises from a water heater, such as popping or rumbling, typically indicate sediment buildup at the bottom of the tank. Flushing the tank can help eliminate the noise.

The ideal temperature setting for a water heater is usually around 120°F (49°C) for safety and efficiency.

To improve efficiency, insulate the water heater tank and pipes, regularly flush the tank to remove sediment, and consider installing a timer to reduce standby heat loss.

Most water heaters last between 8 to 12 years. You should consider replacing it if it is older, requires frequent repairs, or if there's a noticeable decrease in hot water supply.
