Frequently Asked Questions

A water softener is a system that removes minerals like calcium and magnesium from water, which cause hardness. Installing one prevents scale buildup in pipes and appliances, improves soap efficiency, and extends the lifespan of plumbing systems.

The size of the water softener you need depends on the hardness level of your water and your household water usage. It's typically measured in grains per gallon (GPG). A water softener professional can help you calculate the right size based on these factors.

It is possible to install a water softener yourself if you have plumbing experience and follow the manufacturer's instructions carefully. However, hiring a professional can ensure the installation is done correctly and efficiently, helping to avoid potential issues.

The frequency of adding salt to your water softener can vary based on usage and the type of softener. Generally, you should check the salt level monthly and keep the brine tank at least half full of salt. A more precise schedule can be established based on your specific water softener and household water usage.

Regular maintenance for a water softener includes checking salt levels, cleaning the brine tank and resin bed, and occasionally adding a cleaner to the system. Additionally, it's advisable to have a professional inspect the system annually to ensure it operates effectively.
