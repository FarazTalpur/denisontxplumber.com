Frequently Asked Questions

Common causes of copper pipe leaks include corrosion, high water pressure, and faulty installation. Over time, copper pipes can also develop pinhole leaks due to the natural aging process.

You can temporarily fix a leaking copper pipe by using a pipe repair clamp or applying epoxy putty designed for plumbing repairs. These methods can provide a short-term solution until a permanent repair can be made.

To repair a copper pipe, you will need a pipe cutter, soldering torch, flux, solder, sandpaper or brush for cleaning, and safety gear. Depending on the repair type, additional tools like a pipe wrench or pipe repair clamp may be necessary.

Whether you can repair a copper pipe yourself depends on your experience and the complexity of the repair. Simple fixes like replacing a small section might be manageable for DIY enthusiasts, but more complicated issues should be handled by a professional plumber.

To prevent future leaks in copper pipes, consider maintaining optimal water pressure, inspecting and replacing corroded pipe sections, and using water softeners if your water is highly acidic. Regular inspections by a professional plumber can also help identify potential issues early.
